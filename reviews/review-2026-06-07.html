Literature Review Strategies for Medical Researchers

Published: 2026-06-07  |  Journal: Global Journal of Medical Research  |  Publisher: Knowledge Framework Consulting  |  Author: Abhishek Bansal

Introduction

A well‑executed literature review is the foundation of any research project. This article provides practical strategies for conducting efficient and comprehensive literature searches in the medical field.

Defining the Research Question

Use the PICO framework (Population, Intervention, Comparison, Outcome) to formulate a clear and answerable question. This guides the search strategy and prevents irrelevant results.

Database Selection

PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science are essential databases for medical research. Additionally, consider specialized databases like the Cochrane Library for systematic reviews and Embase for pharmacological studies.

Search Techniques

Combine MeSH terms with free‑text keywords to maximize retrieval. Use Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT) to refine searches. Document your search strategy for reproducibility.

Conclusion

Adopting a systematic approach to literature review not only improves the quality of research but also saves time and reduces the risk of missing important studies.
