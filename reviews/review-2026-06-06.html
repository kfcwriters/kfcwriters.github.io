Common Mistakes in Case Report Writing and How to Avoid Them

Published: 2026-06-06  |  Journal: Global Journal of Medical Research  |  Publisher: Knowledge Framework Consulting  |  Author: Abhishek Bansal

Introduction

Case reports remain a valuable tool for sharing unique clinical observations. However, many submissions are rejected due to avoidable errors. This review identifies the most common mistakes in case report writing and provides practical guidance for improvement.

Mistake 1: Lack of a Clear Clinical Question

Every case report should address a specific clinical question. Without a focused objective, the manuscript appears unstructured. Authors should clearly state why the case is novel or instructive.

Mistake 2: Incomplete Patient History and Examination

Omitting relevant clinical details weakens the report. Provide a chronological narrative that includes all pertinent findings, diagnostic tests, and treatment interventions.

Mistake 3: Poor Discussion of the Literature

Many authors fail to place their case within the existing literature. A thorough literature review demonstrates how the case adds to current knowledge.

Conclusion

By addressing these common pitfalls, authors can enhance the quality of their case reports and improve the likelihood of acceptance in peer‑reviewed journals.
